ParaDocs Health

A Multivariate Modeling Toolkit to Identify, Predict, & Mitigate Physician Bias Associated with Clinical Decision Making in Patients Diagnosed with Ketoacidosis

Some more text if wanted

Abstract

This work proposes a technique (named entity recognition (NER), sentiment analysis, decision trees, random forests, and support vector machines (SVM) to detect and minimize implicit bias in de-identified free-text clinical discharge notes for ketoacidosis patients. The bias detection tool reconstructs clinical events from numerous healthcare contexts and searches for pre-determined biased terms, phrases, and terminology connected to care discrepancy according to patient medical history, demographic identifiers, or patient appointment emotion. Board-certified physicians blindly tagged 526 free-text clinical discharge notes and awarded a binary outcome based on prejudice and/or treatment activities that varied from normal medical procedures. Concurrent data ingestion can analyze retrospective and prospective biases, although the study used cross-sectional MIMIC-IV V2.2 de-identified clinical discharge records. The tool's deliberate design detects and eliminates implicit bias in clinical notes, allowing providers to see each patient's care path from a totally reconstructed timeline of clinical events. The technology publicly scores each word for language prejudice to avoid it from affecting clinical decision-making. Language prejudice detection improves clinical note writing, patient care, and healthcare AI/ML models.
